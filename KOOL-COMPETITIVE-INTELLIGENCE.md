# KOOL App — Competitive Intelligence Report
## by Manu 🔍
*Research Director, The Koolture Group | March 2026*

### THE 10 UNIVERSAL PAIN POINTS

1. **The Frankenstack Problem** — planners use 4–7 disconnected tools. 30% of their time is copying data between apps.

2. **Vendor Communication is a Black Hole** — critical communications in email threads nobody can search. #1 cause of day-of disasters.

3. **Budget Tools That Lie** — static calculators. Can't handle deposits vs. balances, escalation, last-minute changes, or automatic alerts.

4. **Guests Are Database Entries** — RSVPs collected, relationships never built. Guest experience ends at confirmation email.

5. **Day-Of Coordination is an Orphan** — *"The day of the event, I abandon every app I own."* (Reddit r/eventplanning). No real-time coordination layer.

6. **Templates Are Generic** — blank checklists. No contextual intelligence (50-person gala ≠ 500-person conference).

7. **No Institutional Memory** — every event starts from zero. Vendor performance, timeline learnings, real spend — all lost.

8. **Client Collaboration is Broken** — portals are read-only. Clients text planners directly because the portal doesn't work.

9. **Mobile is an Afterthought** — every tool except Partiful/Luma is desktop-first. Planners are on phones on event day.

10. **Pricing Doesn't Scale Fairly** — per-seat or per-ticket models penalize success. *"I pay $150/month for Planning Pod and use 20% of it."*

### BOTTOM LINE

The event planning software market is fragmented, desktop-heavy, and emotionally tone-deaf. Planners cobble together 4–7 tools because no single platform has solved the full problem — from first conversation to post-event debrief. KOOL's opportunity is to be the first app that treats events as experiences and planners as creative professionals, not project managers. The day-of coordination gap alone — which every competitor ignores — is a standalone product waiting to be built.
